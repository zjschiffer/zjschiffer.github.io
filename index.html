I am a Ph.D. student in EECS at MIT, advised by Prof. Devavrat Shah in the Laboratory for Information and Decision Systems. My research uses tools from mathematical statistics and economics to study the relationship between performance and social responsibility in machine learning. My work lies at the intersection of machine learning theory and social good (also known as AI ethics).

I have previously worked in intelligent transportation, communication networks, reinforcement learning, and robotics. During my master's, I performed research on autonomous vehicles (a.k.a. self-driving cars) with Prof. Paul Newman at the Unversity of Oxford. As an undergraduate, I studied control & decision systems with Prof. Naomi Leonard at Princeton University.

Teaching

Electrochemical Energy Systems (10.626) | Teaching assistant | Karthish Manthiram
Spring 2017

"The course aims to develop understanding in the area of electrochemical energy systems by building on existing knowledge of thermodynamics, kinetics, and transport."

This was the second year that the course was taught by Prof. Manthiram, and along with my fellow TA, we developed from scratch a majority of the homework problems and exam problems. I also held weekly office hours to answer questions and discuss material in depth with the students. Beyond the course material, I wrote a brief discussion of cell potentials and references based on fundamental chemical thermodynamics. During the semester, I also designed and delivered a lecture on how a potentiostat works.
